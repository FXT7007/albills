Frequently Asked Questions

Why do exchange rates change?
Exchange rates are determined by supply and demand in global currency markets (forex). They are affected by interest rates, inflation, economic growth, political stability, and market sentiment. Gulf currencies pegged to the USD are an exception.
What is the USD to AED exchange rate?
The UAE Dirham has been pegged to the US Dollar at 3.6725 AED per USD since 1997. This means 1 USD = 3.6725 AED. This rate is fixed and does not fluctuate like other currencies.
How do I convert invoice amounts between currencies?
To convert: divide the amount by the exchange rate (or multiply, depending on the currency pair). Use our calculator above for instant, accurate conversions. For official invoices, use your bank's rate on the invoice date.
