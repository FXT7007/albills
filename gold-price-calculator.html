How to Calculate Gold Value

The value of gold depends on three factors: the current gold spot price, the weight of gold, and its purity (karat). Our calculator handles all three automatically.

Gold Purity (Karat) Explained

  • 24 Karat (999.9) — Pure gold, 99.99% gold content. Most expensive per gram.
  • 22 Karat (916.7) — 91.67% gold, commonly used for jewelry in the Gulf region.
  • 18 Karat (750) — 75% gold, popular in European jewelry.
  • 14 Karat (585) — 58.5% gold, common in US jewelry.
  • 9 Karat (375) — 37.5% gold, common in UK jewelry.

Weight Units

  • Troy Ounce — Standard unit for gold trading (31.1 grams)
  • Gram — Most common for small purchases
  • Tola — Traditional South Asian unit (11.66 grams)
💡 Gold prices fluctuate constantly. The price shown is a reference price. Always check live prices from a trusted gold dealer or exchange before making transactions.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the gold price today?
Gold prices change every second during market hours. The price shown in our calculator is a reference price in USD per troy ounce. For real-time trading prices, check sources like the Dubai Gold & Commodities Exchange (DGCX), London Bullion Market Association (LBMA) or your local gold dealer.
How is gold priced?
Gold is priced globally in USD per troy ounce. One troy ounce equals 31.1034 grams. The price is determined by supply and demand in global markets and fluctuates continuously during trading hours.
What is the best karat for investment?
24K gold is preferred for investment as it contains the highest gold content (99.99%). Gold bars and coins used for investment are typically 24K. 22K is popular for jewelry in the UAE and Gulf region.
How much is 1 gram of gold in AED?
To calculate the price of 1 gram of gold in AED: divide the gold spot price (in USD per troy oz) by 31.1034 to get the price per gram in USD, then multiply by the USD/AED exchange rate (approximately 3.6725). Use our calculator above for instant results.
