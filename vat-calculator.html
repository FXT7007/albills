How to Use the VAT Calculator

Our free VAT calculator makes it simple to calculate VAT for any country in seconds. Here is how to use it:

Adding VAT to a price (exclusive VAT)

If you have a net price (before VAT) and need to calculate the final price including VAT:

  • Select your country or enter a custom VAT rate
  • Enter the net amount in the Amount field
  • Select "Add VAT to price" from the Calculation Type
  • The calculator instantly shows net amount, VAT amount, and total

Extracting VAT from a total (inclusive VAT)

If you have a total price that includes VAT and need to find the net amount and VAT portion:

  • Select your country or enter a custom VAT rate
  • Enter the total amount (including VAT)
  • Select "Extract VAT from total" from the Calculation Type
  • The calculator shows what portion is net price and what is VAT

VAT Formula

Adding VAT: Total = Net Price × (1 + VAT Rate / 100)

Extracting VAT: Net Price = Total Price / (1 + VAT Rate / 100)

VAT Amount: VAT = Total Price − Net Price

Frequently Asked Questions

What is VAT?
VAT (Value Added Tax) is a consumption tax placed on goods and services at each stage of production or distribution. Businesses collect VAT on behalf of the government and can reclaim VAT paid on business expenses. In the UAE it is called VAT at 5%, in Saudi Arabia it is 15%, and in the UK it is 20%.
What is the VAT rate in the UAE?
The UAE introduced VAT at a standard rate of 5% on 1 January 2018. This applies to most goods and services. Some categories are zero-rated (0%) including certain food items, healthcare and education, and some are exempt including financial services and residential properties.
What is the VAT rate in Saudi Arabia?
Saudi Arabia's VAT rate was increased from 5% to 15% on 1 July 2020 as part of fiscal measures. This applies to most goods and services. Essential goods, healthcare and education may be zero-rated or exempt.
How do I calculate VAT in the UAE?
To calculate UAE VAT at 5%: multiply the net price by 1.05 to get the total including VAT. For example, AED 100 × 1.05 = AED 105 total. To extract VAT from a total: divide by 1.05. For example, AED 105 ÷ 1.05 = AED 100 net, and AED 5 is the VAT.
